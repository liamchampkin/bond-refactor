How do I start a test?

  • Click on your subject, (Maths, Verbal Reasoning or Non-verbal reasoning)
  • Click on a topic from the list of tests
  • Click Start Test
  • Follow instructions to answer each question
  • Click Next to move to the next question

How do I see which questions I got right?

  • At the end of the test, click Finish now and see my marks
  • The time of taking your test and your score (percentage %) will appear in the title bar
  • The test will start at question no.1 again and show a tick or cross against your answer
  • There will be feedback below which explains the correct answer
  • Click Next to move through the questions or click on the question number to go straight to a specific question

Take care: your use of punctuation or decimal places may be taken into account in deciding if your answer is correct. Make sure that you check any punctuation that you use before you submit your test for marking.

What do I do when I have finished checking my answers?

  • Click Close Test at any time
  • Your completed and marked test will appear in the list of Completed tests – you can go back and check your answers again any time you want.

What do the different percentages mean?

You will see percentage marks at 3 different levels:

  • Subject Level (e.g. Maths): shows the overall average for all the tests
    Example: If you have averages of 40%, 30%, 20% for the tests you have done in Maths – your overall average will be 40+30+20 divided by 3 = 30%
  • Named test or topic (e.g. Nets and Cubes): shows the average of the last 3 attempts at that specific test
  • Completed tests: shows the exact mark each time you attempted a test

    • Your best score is the highest mark you got in all your attempts at a particular topic.

What happens if I want to stop but I haven't answered all the questions?

  • Click on Finish later button
  • Your answers will be saved
  • Next time you select the same test, the arrow will say Continue
  • Click on Continue and you will see the questions you answered.

How do I get back to the test I didn't finish before?

  • In My Homepage select the subject
  • Select the name of the test
  • Click on the Continue arrow – you will see the answers you gave last time
  • Go backwards and forwards through the questions as normal.

How many times can I change my answer?

You can change your answers as many times as you like until you click on Finish now and see my marks. Your test will then be saved and you will only be able to check your answers after that. However, you can always have another go at the same test.

How many times can I do a test?

You can do the same test as many times as you like.

Each time you click on Finish now and see my marks it will be added to the list of Completed tests

Will each test be different or the same?

Every time you take a test of the same name it will be different from the one before – there will be some different questions and the order of questions will be different.

While I am taking a test I am redirected to the home screen and logged out

The most likely reason is that you have more than one Internet browser or tab open which is logged in to Bond. Bond works best when you use just one screen at a time.

Did this help? If not then the security settings on your browser may be interfeering with Bond, try using a different Internet browser.

If this did not help then contact our support team and let us know your username and the Internet browser that you are using.

Will I only ever get correct or incorrect for an answer?

You will get feedback when you have answered questions and had your test marked. This feedback should help you understand why you got the question right or wrong.

What if I don't understand the question?

  1. Look at the example question for the test. This will give you an idea of the type of question in the test.
  2. Try out different answers (do the same test again and again) and check which answers you got correct and which you didn't.
  3. Read the feedback against your marked answers. This should help you understand why your answer was right or wrong.

The more you do the tests, the more you will get used to the different types of questions and the answers they are looking for.

How do I get back from a completed test to the list of topics or subjects?

You will see a "crumbtrail" in blue at the top of the page. For example, iIt may say: My home page – Verbal Reasoning – test

  • Click on Verbal Reasoning to go back to the list of tests
  • Click on My home page to go back to the list of subjects

Wherever you see the blue "crumbtrail" you can click on any part of it to move around the pages.
